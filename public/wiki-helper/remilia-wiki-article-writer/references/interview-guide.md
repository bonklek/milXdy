# Interview Guide

Use these questions selectively based on the article type. Ask 3-6 at a time.

## Universal Questions

- What should the page title be?
- What is the shortest neutral definition of the topic?
- Why does this belong on Remilia Wiki?
- What are the strongest sources or links?
- What dates matter: origin, launch, event, peak activity, retirement, aftermath?
- Which people, groups, accounts, projects, or scenes are directly connected?
- Which existing Remilia Wiki pages should be linked?
- Are there aliases, alternate spellings, handles, tickers, or nicknames?
- What claims are certain, and what claims are uncertain or disputed?

## Concept, Phrase, Or Meme

- Where did the phrase or concept originate?
- Who coined or popularized it?
- What does it mean in plain language?
- What influences or adjacent ideas shaped it?
- How was it used in posts, art, Discords, collections, or wiki discourse?
- Did the meaning change over time?

## Event Or Scene

- When and where did it happen?
- Who participated or organized it?
- What led up to it?
- What happened during the event?
- What changed afterward?
- Was there controversy, misattribution, or later reinterpretation?

## Person Or Social Media Personality

- What names, handles, or aliases are relevant?
- What are they known for?
- What projects, writings, posts, or aesthetics are they associated with?
- What is their relationship to Remilia or adjacent scenes?
- What dates define their activity?
- Which claims need careful attribution?

## Project, Artwork, Collection, Or Website

- Who created it?
- When did it launch?
- What medium, platform, chain, website, or repository is involved?
- What are its visual, conceptual, technical, or community features?
- What related projects came before or after?
- What official links should appear in External links?

## Token

- What is the ticker and full name?
- When did it launch?
- What chain and contract are relevant?
- What role does it play in the ecosystem?
- Were there presales, airdrops, tokenomics, or official announcements?
- Which financial claims should be avoided unless sourced?

## Draft Review Checklist

- Opening sentence defines the topic without hype.
- Dates and names are checked.
- First mentions of related pages are internally linked.
- Unsupported claims are removed, softened, or marked for citation.
- References use wiki citation templates when possible.
- The final output is valid MediaWiki wikitext, not Markdown.
